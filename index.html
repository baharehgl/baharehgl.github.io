Background

I’m a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Portland State University, advised by Dr. Banafsheh Rekabdar in the Artificial Intelligence Lab.

My research focuses on developing adaptive, intelligent anomaly detection systems for time series data by integrating reinforcement learning, generative models, and large language models. By combining dynamic reward scaling, semantic reasoning through LLMs, and continual learning mechanisms, I aim to create systems that are not only accurate but also interpretable, efficient, and capable of adapting to evolving real-world environments. My work addresses critical challenges in industrial monitoring, healthcare, finance, and cybersecurity; domains where detecting anomalies early can prevent catastrophic failures and save lives.
